# Record brand profile

Status: product direction. Name, mark, domains, and app-store listing still require clearance before public launch.

Customer name: **Record**
Repository and self-host distribution: **Personal Server**
Promise: **Keep the life you live useful.**
Category: private personal record system.

## Product character

Record is calm, exact, private, and capable. It helps a person capture, continue, inspect, and correct the facts of daily life. It is neither a motivational coach nor a generic “life operating system.”

The brand’s organizing metaphor is the register: a dependable sequence of records with visible source, time, state, and next action. The indexed-spine mark carries that idea into the app icon and navigation.

## Voice

Use direct, factual language.

- “Detected from a Revolut notification.”
- “Set 2 matches last week.”
- “Episode 4 of 12.”
- “Last checked 4 minutes ago.”

Avoid unsupported certainty, productivity guilt, praise filler, faux intimacy, “AI-powered” as a substitute for behavior, and vague status such as “All synced” when a timestamp or source is available.

## Visual world

Graphite planes, cool lines, Sora interface type, JetBrains Mono data, and one electric violet accent. Success, warning, and danger colors are semantic. Cash, Gym, Habits, Music, Series, and Assistant are instruments in one Record product, not visual sub-brands.

The register is the core composition. Rows, dividers, timelines, and focused workbenches are preferred to walls of floating cards. Artwork and charts appear when they carry information. Shadows belong to overlays; normal hierarchy comes from spacing, planes, and rules.

Imagery shows the real product: a transaction under review, a workout set, a habit decision, an artist ranking, or a season and episode row. Do not use stock lifestyle photography, abstract AI meshes, decorative orbits, fake life scores, glassmorphism, or fabricated customer activity presented as real.

## Portfolio

- **Record**: customer web and Android application and managed service.
- **Personal Server**: source repository and personal self-host distribution.
- Cash, Gym, Habits, Music, Series, and Assistant: product records, never separate brands.
- Household or shared plans remain reserved until permissions and private/shared boundaries are designed.

## Commercial posture

The managed service is the primary customer offer. Personal noncommercial self-hosting remains possible. Commercial hosting or resale requires a separate agreement. Final license text, trademarks, domains, app-store name, and claims require legal review before launch.
